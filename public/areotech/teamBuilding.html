Life is either an incredible adventure...or nothing at all



Fly fighter jets and be a fighter pilot for a day


You don't have to be a famous celebrity to experience the thrill of starring in your own documentary film. All cameras will be on you as you prepare for and complete a flight in a jet fighter. Flying adventures in the L-39 jet fighter are now available in the town where dreams come true.


Make your dream true and fly a jet today, you will never feel the same again.




The movie opens with footage of you in the hangar, being briefed on your mission. We then see you interacting with the ground crew as you make your way to the flightline. Multiple camera angles capture the intensity on your face as you strap into the cockpit of a legendary jet trainer and await the go ahead from the tower. You look amazingly like a real fighter pilot. We watch on camera as you take off from Los Angeles, climbing at a rate of over 4000 feet per minute. The camera turns to show the great view you have of Hollywood and the Los Angeles basin from the air. Multiple camera angles soon capture the change in scenery. There, above the cacti and tumbleweed, we see you experiencing loops, rolls, Immelmans and more. We almost feel like we're in the cockpit with you, controlling a full-throttle military jet with your fingertips.


You're not just the star of this short film. You share screenwriting credits. Each flight is designed to match your taste for adrenaline. Each script prepared to meet your expectations. You determine what maneuvers you'd like to perform and whether you'll make low passes through the canyons or fly low over the ocean.


We know how important making this film is to you. That's why we've assembled an incredible group of people to help you. Our film production team has won five Emmy Awards for "Excellence in Television Production" and has been actively involved in the making of many Hollywood movies. You'll be working with the same people and flying in the same plane that was used in the making of the major motion picture JARHEAD.


Course of the Experience – fly the L-39 in LA:


  • Welcome at the airbase
  • Security briefing on the base
  • Meet the team - your pilot, mechanics and the rest of the crew
  • Get dressed: G suit, helmet
  • Pilot briefing and flight plan: the pilot explains how the fighter jet works, the Gs, the accelerations, but also the flight plan, weather conditions…
  • Instrument briefing in the cockpit
  • Be seated… you get strapped into the cockpit 25mn before take off. Your mechanic teaches how the ejection seat works and rehearses procedures with you.
  • Engines on… and take off!
